Sinigang

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. beef short rib
  • 2 (25g) packs Knorr Sinigang na may Gabi Recipe Mix
  • 2 cups fresh watermelon, sliced and seeds removed
  • 1 bunch kangkong leaves, stalk cut into serving pieces
  • 12 to 15 pieces snake beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 medium tomato, wedged
  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced
  • 8 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 4 tablespoons fish sauce

Procedure:

  1. Fill a pressure cooker with water then bring to a boil.
  2. Add the onion and tomato. Boil for a further five minutes.
  3. In the pressure cooker, place the short rib of beef, cover it, then cook under pressure for half an hour.
  4. Take off the pressure cooker's lid. Watermelon should be added. Boil for another 10 to 12 minutes.
  5. Add the recipe mix for Knorr Sinigang na may Gabi. Stir. Cook for three minutes.
  6. Include the snake beans. Cook for five minutes.
  7. Add fish sauce and ground black pepper and stir.
  8. Place the stem and leaves of the kangkong in the pot. Cover. Give it five to eight minutes.
  9. Move to a bowl for serving and enjoy!

Kare-Kare

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs oxtail, cut in 2-inch slices (you can also use tripe or beef slices)
  • 1 piece small banana flower bud, sliced
  • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy
  • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2-inch slices
  • 4 pieces eggplants, sliced
  • 1 cup ground peanuts
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup shrimp paste
  • 34 ounces water (about 1 liter)
  • 1/2 cup annatto seeds, soaked in a cup of water
  • 1/2 cup toasted ground rice
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a big pot.
  2. Add the oxtail and onions, then simmer for two and a half to three hours, or until the oxtail is cooked (35 minutes if using a pressure cooker).
  3. After the meat is soft, simmer it for five to seven minutes with the addition of ground peanuts, peanut butter, and coloring (water from the annatto seed mixture).
  4. Saut√© the garlic in a different skillet, then add the string beans, eggplant, and banana blossom. Cook for five minutes.
  5. Move the cooked veggies to the big pot with the other ingredients.
  6. To taste, add salt and pepper. Serve while still hot and have fun!
